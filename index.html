This map shows the correlation between bike share access and bike use by commuters in New York City. As Citi Bike's system expands, the percentage of commuters that bike to work increases. The map compares commuter behavior in 2014 and 2020. Citi Bike station locations are from 2013 and 2019 respectively to allow time for commuter behavior to reflect new station access. Correlation is not causation - access to the Citi Bike system is not the only variable that influences how people commute or decide to use bikes.

Some data from 2020 is missing, leaving a few 2020 census tracts blank.

Explore how access to bike share stations correlates with cycling behavior.

Move around the map and zoom in to explore certain tracts and their neighborhoods. Toggle between 2020 and 2014 to see the Citi Bike system expand and cyclist behavior change. Click on any tract to get specific details on the share of commuters that bike, take the subway or bus, or walk.
